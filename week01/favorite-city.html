Indianapolis, Indiana

Indianapolis, colloquially known as Indy, is the capital and most populous city in the state of Indiana. It is located in the central part of the state and is affectionately nicknamed the "Crossroads of America" due to the numerous interstate highways that intersect there.

The city is a major hub for sports, most famously known for hosting the Indianapolis 500, which is the largest single-day sporting event in the world. Beyond its racing heritage, Indianapolis features a vibrant downtown area centered around Monument Circle, a sprawling park system, and the largest children's museum in the world.
